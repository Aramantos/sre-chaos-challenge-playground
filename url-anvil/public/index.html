URL Anvil

Enter one or more URLs (one per line) to run a simple performance test against them.

Purpose of URL Anvil:

The URL Anvil is a micro-benchmark service designed to test the performance of external URLs. It simulates a workload by fetching multiple URLs concurrently and performing a CPU-bound task (hashing their combined content). This service acts as a standardized target for the SRE Chaos Challenges, allowing contributors to measure and optimize their services against a consistent workload.

How to Use for Challenges:

  • Crash Script Challenge: Use the URL Anvil to find the breaking point of a service. By testing a large number of URLs or very slow URLs, you can observe how your service handles stress and identify its maximum RPS before failure.
  • Robust Service Challenge: Integrate the URL Anvil into your service's testing pipeline. The `total_fetch_time_ms` and `average_fetch_time_ms` metrics can help you evaluate the efficiency and responsiveness of your service under load. Aim for consistent and low fetch times.
  • Basic Usage: Enter URLs in the text area, click "Test URLs" to run a benchmark, or "Load Sample URLs" for a quick start.

Output Explanation:

  • urls_tested: The number of URLs successfully fetched and processed.
  • total_fetch_time_ms: The total time in milliseconds taken to fetch all URLs concurrently.
  • average_fetch_time_ms: The average time in milliseconds per URL fetch.
  • content_hash: A SHA-256 hash of the combined content from all fetched URLs. This verifies content integrity.
  • timestamp: The UTC timestamp when the test was completed.
